Introduction to the Diamond Price Dataset Analysis¶

Diamonds are one of the most valuable gemstones in the world, with prices influenced by multiple factors such as carat, /n cut, color, clarity, and depth. Understanding the pricing structure of diamonds is essential for buyers, sellers, and businesses in the jewelry industry.

In this project, I analyze the Diamond Price Dataset from Kaggle, which contains detailed information about over 53,000 diamonds,/n including their physical attributes and corresponding market prices. The objective is to explore patterns in diamond pricing, identify key factors affecting value, and build predictive models to estimate diamond prices accurately.

Objectives of the Project¶

1 Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to uncover insights and trends in diamond pricing.
2 Visualize relationships between diamond attributes and price using various data visualization techniques.
3 Apply feature engineering to transform and optimize data for predictive modeling.
4 Develop machine learning models (XGBoost) to predict diamond prices based on given features.
5 Evaluate model performance using appropriate metrics such as RMSE, R² score, and MAE.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consists of 53,940 observations with the following key features:

Carat – The weight of the diamond (primary factor affecting price).
Cut – The quality of the diamond's cut (Fair, Good, Very Good, Premium, Ideal).
Color – Diamond color, graded from J (worst) to D (best).
Clarity – Measurement of inclusions/flaws (I1, SI2, SI1, ..., IF).
Depth & Table – Proportions affecting brilliance.
Price – The target variable (price in US dollars).
Why This Project Matters?
This project provides valuable insights into the pricing structure of diamonds, helping jewelers, investors, and customers make data-driven decisions. By building an accurate pricing model, we can predict the value of diamonds based on their characteristics, leading to better transparency in the diamond market.
